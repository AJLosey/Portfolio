About Me

I highly recommend that everyone listen to the 2016 album "Spirit Phone" by Lemon Demon. There's a song called "Cabinet Man" about a living arcade machine that has two lines that reference the game "Polybius". For those that don't know, Polybius is an equally fictional arcade machine allegedly deployed by some deep-government-psy-ops that drove kids insane. "Lemon Demon," by the way, is a band with only one official member, the musical genius called Neil Cicierega. Another one of my favorite songs in the album is "Spiral of Ants". This masterpiece describes a real phenomenon where ants accidentally lay down guiding pheremones in a circle. Ants can get trapped in this circle, following their own trails, and laying down more pheremones as they go. Other ants get sucked in, the problem worsens, and eventually massive swarms can get caught up in this lethal feedback loop. It's insane, and fascinating.

Insect patterns and video game conspiracies are a pretty good summary of "about me," but for the record, I am a student in the full-stack development bootcamp at University of Texas. I live in the South Austin area. I enjoy javascript and c#, and have some familiarity with html, css, jquery, and bootstrap. When I'm not learning code, I'm either learning something about biology, learning about or playing video games, writing, or I'm asleep.
